## 2. Platform Community Engagement Benchmarks (REAL DATA)

### Discord
- **200M MAU** globally, **690M+ registered users**
- **94 min/day** average user time (heavy users: 4+ hours)
- **26.5M DAU**, engagement ratio ~10.2%
- **19M+ active servers**
- Demographics: 67.7% male / 32.3% female, core 25-34 age group
- US = largest user base
- **Key community ops reality**: Most servers have <100 active members. Top 1% servers capture majority of activity. Server churn is high — most servers die within 3 months without active management.

### TikTok
- **1.59B MAU** globally (2025)
- **15.8M creators** analyzed (by ClickAnalytic 2026)
- **Engagement rate by views: 4.20%** (2026 benchmark, 9% YoY increase)
- **18% engagement rate** for US influencers (Statista)
- **7.4%-8.1%** average engagement rate (Influencer Marketing Factory)
- **55% of Gen Z** engage with brand content on TikTok daily
- **Algorithm shift (2026)**: prioritizes community-aligned content over viral hits. Niche relevance > broad reach.

### YouTube
- **2.5B+ MAU**
- Community Tab: used by creators for polls, images, updates (not a primary community platform)
- Memberships: recurring revenue via channel memberships ($0.99-$99.99/mo)
- **Different community model** vs Chinese platforms: creator-centric, less peer-to-peer interaction

### Instagram
- **2B+ MAU**
- Broadcast Channels: one-to-many messaging for creators (added 2023)
- Close Friends: private stories for smaller groups
- Organic engagement rate: ~**0.6%** (varies by industry)
- Community building happens mainly in DMs and Stories, less in feed

### Reddit
- **1.1B+ MAU** (2026, includes logged-out users)
- **100K+ active subreddits**
- Key differentiator: **interest-based, not follower-based**. Communities around topics, not people.
- Moderation culture: community-run, strict rules, heavy automation (AutoMod)
- Best platform for: niche interest communities, Q&A, discussion, support

### Substack/Newsletter
- **3M+ paid subscriptions** (across all writers)
- Top conversion rate: **free→paid 5-10%** typical
- Top performers: Lenny's Newsletter (~$400K/yr), Letters from an American, The Diff, Slow Boring
- Pricing range: **$5-$20/mo** or **$50-$200/yr** typical for paid tiers
- Key community ops: newsletter IS the community touchpoint; comments section + chat are secondary

## 3. Western Community Operations Terminology (Standard Usage)

| Term | Meaning | Used In |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Member Onboarding** | The process of welcoming new members and guiding them to first value | Every platform |
| **Welcome Sequence** | Automated series of messages/emails for new members | Discord, Circle, Email |
| **Community Guidelines** | Rules of conduct | Every platform |
| **Moderation** | Enforcing community guidelines, content review | Every platform |
| **Channels** | Categorized discussion spaces (topic-based) | Discord, Slack, Circle |
| **Threads** | Sub-conversations within channels | Discord, Slack, Reddit |
| **Roles / Tags** | Member identity/access markers | Discord, Circle, Reddit |
| **Server / Space / Group** | A community instance | Discord (Server), Circle (Space), Skool (Group) |
| **Member Tiers** | Free / Paid / VIP levels | Circle, Skool, Patreon |
| **Community Champions** | Volunteer power users who help manage | Enterprise communities |
| **Community-Led Growth (CLG)** | Growth driven by community engagement | SaaS, Creator businesses |
| **Retention / Churn** | Member retention rate / dropout rate | Paid communities, SaaS |
| **Activation / "Aha moment"** | The point where a new member gets value | Onboarding design |
| **NPS (Net Promoter Score)** | Member satisfaction metric | Enterprise communities |
| **DAU/MAU ratio** | Daily vs monthly active user ratio | Platform health metric |
| **Referral / Member-get-Member** | Existing members invite new ones | Growth strategy |
| **Seeding / Ambassador Program** | Strategic early members who set culture | Launch strategy |
| **DM (Direct Message)** | Private 1-on-1 conversation | Discord, Instagram, X |
| **Pinned Post / Announcement** | Sticky message at top of channel | Discord, Reddit, Circle |
| **FAQ / Knowledge Base** | Self-service answers | Community health |

## 4. Western Community Lifecycle (Standard Model)

```
Discovery → Onboarding → Activation → Engagement → Retention → Advocacy
    |            |            |            |            |            |
  Search       Welcome     First        Regular      Renewal     Referral
  Referral     Sequence    Interaction  Participation Sub         Testimonial
  Social                        |                         |
  Ads                      "Aha moment"               Churn risk
```

**Key differences from Chinese model:**
- Western: content-first, then community (join for content, stay for community)
- Chinese: relationship-first, then content (join for guanxi/connection, content is secondary)
- Western onboarding: 3-7 email sequence + in-platform guided tour
- Chinese onboarding: 红包 (red packet) + 群规 + immediate personal follow

## 5. Platform-Specific Operational Standards

### Discord Server Structure (Common Best Practice)
```
#welcome        → Member onboarding, roles, rules
#announcements  → Admin-only posts (read-only for members)
#general        → Open chat
#introductions  → New members introduce themselves
#resources      → Pinned guides, FAQs
#events         → Upcoming activities
#feedback       → Member suggestions
#showcase       → Member work/products
#random         → Off-topic chat
[voice channels] → Voice chat rooms
```

### Circle/Skool Typical Pricing
- **$9-19/mo** for basic content access
- **$29-49/mo** for community + weekly calls
- **$99-199/mo** for VIP/coaching tier
- Annual plans: discount equivalent to 2 months free

### TikTok Community Building Strategy
- Post 1-3x/day minimum
- Respond to ALL comments within 2 hours (algorithm boost)
- Use Duet/Stitch for community collaboration
- Live streaming 2-3x/week for real-time engagement
- Niche communities (BookTok, FinTok, etc.) have higher engagement

## 6. Key Adaptation Rules for Chinese→Western Community Content

1. **No WeChat references** — replace with Discord/Circle/Slack equivalents
2. **No 红包 (red packet)** — replace with referral discounts, free trials, content upgrades
3. **No "群托" (seeded members)** — replace with ambassador programs, power user initiatives
4. **Hourly schedule → Weekly rhythm** — Western communities don't do scheduled hourly posts
5. **朋友圈 content → Newsletter/Social media content calendar**
6. **私域 → Owned audience** — "private domain" is not a known concept
7. **SOP → Playbook or Standard Operating Procedures** (SOP is known in business contexts)
8. **裂变 → Referral/Viral/Loop** — use the standard growth marketing terms
9. **KOL → Creator/Influencer** — not key opinion leader
10. **社群 → Community** — simple, direct translation works here
