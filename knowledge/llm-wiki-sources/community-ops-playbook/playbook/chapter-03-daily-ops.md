# Chapter 3: Daily Operations Playbook

**Purpose:** Consistency is what separates a thriving community from one that flames out after six weeks. This chapter gives you a weekly rhythm, content pillars, and moderation workflows that keep the engine running without burning you out.

---

## Quick Wins — Do These Today

1. **Set up your daily themes.** Write Monday through Friday into your content calendar and assign one theme per day (Educational, Spotlight, Interactive, Culture, Roundup).
2. **Write your community rules (3-5).** Post them in your #welcome or #rules channel. Keep them short and specific. Pin them.
3. **Block 30 minutes per day on your calendar.** Same time, every day. This is your community operations block. Don't skip it.

---

## Why a Weekly Rhythm Beats an Hourly Schedule

Some community playbooks try to script your entire day — post at 9 AM, engage at 11 AM, moderate at 2 PM, review at 5 PM. That might work for someone whose full-time job is running a single community group. It doesn't work for you.

You're probably:

- Running a business alongside the community
- Managing multiple communities
- Doing this part-time while holding down a job
- A team of one

A minute-by-minute schedule will break on day three. What you need instead is a weekly rhythm — a predictable pattern that your members can rely on and you can execute without thinking.

The principle is simple: **each day has a theme.** Members know what to expect. You know what to prepare. Content flows naturally instead of feeling forced.

---

## The Daily Theme Framework

Here's the core weekly structure adapted from proven engagement patterns:

| Day | Theme | Purpose |
|-----|-------|---------|
| Monday | Educational Monday | Teach something valuable |
| Tuesday | Spotlight Tuesday | Celebrate member wins |
| Wednesday | Interactive Wednesday | Get members talking |
| Thursday | Culture Thursday | Share behind-the-scenes, build identity |
| Friday | Roundup Friday | Recap the week, preview next week |
| Saturday | Light Weekend (optional) | Casual, off-topic |
| Sunday | Light Weekend (optional) | Rest or low-effort prompt |

Let's walk through each day in detail.

### Monday: Educational Monday

The goal is to start the week with value. Give members something they can use immediately.

**Content ideas:**

- **Tutorial or walkthrough:** A step-by-step guide related to your community's focus. For a game dev community: "How to optimize your pixel art for mobile screens."
- **Resource roundup:** 3-5 curated links, tools, or articles. Keep commentary short and useful.
- **Framework or template:** Share a fill-in-the-blank template members can use. Example: "Here's my game design doc template — steal it."
- **Expert highlight:** Share a clip, quote, or insight from someone in the field. Could be you or someone else.
- **"Ask Me Anything" prompt:** Drop a topic and invite questions throughout the day.

**How to post it:**

- **Discord:** Create a thread in #educational or #resources. Pin the best responses.
- **Circle:** Post in the "Learning" or "Resources" space with a descriptive title.
- **Skool:** Schedule it as a lesson or post in the main classroom feed.

**Don't overproduce it.** A 3-paragraph post with a screenshot works better than a perfectly designed graphic that takes two hours to make. Consistency beats polish.

### Tuesday: Spotlight Tuesday

This is your most important engagement day. Spotlight Tuesday shifts the focus from you to your members. People stay in communities where they feel seen.

**Content ideas:**

- **Member win of the week:** "Congrats to @marcus for shipping his first mobile game! Here's a link to check it out. Marcus, what was the hardest part?"
- **New member shoutout:** Highlight 2-3 new members who completed their intros. Welcome them specifically and encourage others to say hi.
- **Contribution highlight:** "This tip from @jordan about debugging shader code saved me 3 hours this week. Jordan, where'd you learn this?"
- **"Behind the build":** Ask a member to share their current project, even if it's unfinished. The goal is to normalize work-in-progress and encourage sharing.

**Why this matters:**

Communities die when the leader is the only star. Spotlight Tuesday trains members to look at each other instead of at you. It builds the social fabric that keeps people around even when you're offline.

**The nitty-gritty:**

- You'll need to collect wins proactively. Keep a list throughout the week. When someone shares a success in #general, add it to your "Spotlight candidates" document.
- Tag the person you're spotlighting. Ask them 1-2 questions about their experience. This makes the spotlight interactive, not just a broadcast.
- If you have a Circle or Skool community, use member profiles to link to their work.

### Wednesday: Interactive Wednesday

Midweek energy dips. Interactive Wednesday is designed to pull people into the conversation with low-friction prompts.

**Content ideas:**

- **Polls:** "Which game engine do you use? A) Unity B) Unreal C) Godot D) Custom" Polls are the highest-engagement, lowest-effort format. Use them generously.
- **Q&A thread:** Open the floor for any question. No bad questions. Answer as many as you can and let other members answer too.
- **Challenge or prompt:** "Write a 5-line dialog for a character who just lost their save file. Best one gets a shoutout."
- **"Two truths and a lie":** Post yours and invite others to share theirs. Works especially well in communities where members don't know each other well yet.
- **Feedback thread:** "Post your project link. Give feedback to the person above you. Everyone gets at least one review."

**Platform-specific approaches:**

| Platform | Best Interactive Format | Why |
|----------|------------------------|-----|
| Discord | Thread in #general or #discussions | Real-time, easy to jump in |
| Circle | Poll in main feed + threaded discussion | Structured, persistent |
| Skool | Classroom discussion post | Shows in the activity feed |

**Pro tip:** Interactive posts that require low effort (one click, one sentence) get 3-5x more responses than those requiring paragraphs. Layer them: start with a poll, then invite deeper discussion in a thread.

### Thursday: Culture Thursday

This is where you build community identity. Culture Thursday is less about value delivery and more about personality, connection, and shared experience.

**Content ideas:**

- **Behind-the-scenes:** What are you working on? What's frustrating you? What's exciting? Share something real. "Spent 4 hours today refactoring the save system and accidentally deleted my entire player controller. Taking the dog for a walk instead."
- **Team or founder intro:** Your story, your why, your mistakes. Members connect with humans, not brands.
- **Themed thread:** "What's your setup?" (desk photos, favorite tools, workspace tours). "Soundtrack Saturday" (what are you listening to while you work?).
- **Community values check-in:** "One of our values is 'ship before it's perfect.' Who's done that this week? What did you learn?"
- **Inside jokes and traditions:** Create recurring memes, traditions, or callbacks that only community members understand. This is how a group becomes a community.

**Why this matters:**

People don't stay for the content. They stay for the feeling of belonging. Culture Thursday is where that feeling gets built.

**The trap:** Don't force culture. If your community isn't naturally funny, don't force humor. If they're serious professionals, don't force casual banter. Culture Thursday should amplify your community's natural personality, not invent one.

### Friday: Roundup Friday

Close the week by making members feel caught up and ready for next week.

**Content ideas:**

- **Weekly recap:** What happened this week? Top 3 discussions, best resources shared, member wins, any announcements.
- **Upcoming preview:** What's coming next week? Monday's topic, Wednesday's event, any guest appearances.
- **Weekend prompt:** Low-stakes prompt for the weekend. "What's one game you're planning to play this weekend?" or "What's your Sunday reset routine?"
- **Thank you:** A genuine thank you to the community. "This week was wild. Thanks to everyone who helped answer questions in the feedback thread — you're what makes this community great."

**Pro tip:** Use a template for your roundup. Same structure every week. Members will start to look for it. Keep it to 5 bullet points max — don't let it become a chore to write or read.

### Weekend: Light Engagement (Optional)

Weekends are quieter. Don't fight it. Post a very low-effort prompt if you want, but don't expect or demand responses.

**Ideas:**

- "What are you playing/building/reading this weekend?"
- "Share a photo from your weekend (pet edition)"
- Nothing. It's okay to let the community rest.

## Content Pillars

Your daily themes are the *when*. Content pillars are the *what*. Every piece of content you post should fall into one of four categories:

| Pillar | Description | % of Content | Example |
|--------|-------------|--------------|---------|
| **Educational** | Teaches something useful | 40% | Tutorials, guides, frameworks, templates, expert insights |
| **Community** | Highlights members and conversations | 30% | Spotlights, shoutouts, member wins, introductions |
| **Promotional** | Promotes your products or offers | 15% | New course, template launch, referral program, events |
| **Cultural** | Builds identity and connection | 15% | Behind-the-scenes, values, inside jokes, team stories |

The 40/30/15/15 split is a guideline, not a rule. If you're in launch mode, promotional content might spike to 30% temporarily. If you're rebuilding engagement, educational might jump to 50%. What matters is that **promotional content never dominates.** A community that feels like a sales channel will hemorrhage members.

### Planning Your Content Mix

Here's what a month of Mondays (Educational) might look like:

| Week | Topic | Format |
|------|-------|--------|
| 1 | "How to structure a game design document" | Tutorial post + template |
| 2 | "5 tools I use for pixel art" | Resource roundup |
| 3 | "Interview: How @mark shipped his first Steam game" | Q&A with member |
| 4 | "My debugging workflow" | Walkthrough + screenshots |

And Tuesdays (Spotlight):

| Week | Focus | Format |
|------|-------|--------|
| 1 | Member win: first game launch | Spotlight post + Q&A |
| 2 | New member welcome wave | Group shoutout |
| 3 | Contribution highlight: best resource shared this month | Recognition post |
| 4 | Member project showcase | Gallery thread |

## Moderation Workflows

The better your community gets, the more moderation you'll need. Not because people are bad — but because growth attracts attention, and attention attracts noise.

### The Moderation Stack

Three layers, from least to most intervention:

**Layer 1: Preventative (set up before you need it)**

- **Clear rules:** 3-5 rules, written plainly. Pin them in your welcome channel. Example: "Be helpful, not harsh. No self-promotion outside #shameless-plug. Keep it PG-13."
- **Role gates:** New members can only post in #welcome and #introductions for the first 24 hours. This stops drive-by spam and gives you time to vet.
- **Auto-moderation:** Discord's AutoMod catches keywords. Circle and Skool have content moderation filters. Set these up on day one, not after the first spam wave.

**Layer 2: Responsive (handle issues as they come)**

- **The 5-minute rule:** If you see a problematic post, address it within 5 minutes — even if your response is just "I see this, give me a moment to review." Silence reads as approval.
- **Warning → mute → kick → ban.** Progressive discipline, not zero tolerance (except for genuine harassment or threats — those are instant bans).
- **Private conversation first.** If someone breaks a rule, DM them. Most people don't realize they're being a problem, and a private conversation preserves their dignity.

**Layer 3: Escalation (when things get serious)**

| Situation | Response | Timeframe |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Rule violation (minor) | DM warning + link to rules | Within 1 hour |
| Repeated minor violations | 24-hour mute + formal warning | Immediate |
| Harassment or threats | Instant ban + screenshot evidence | Immediate |
| Spam bot | Instant ban + report to platform | Immediate |
| Dispute between members | Temporarily lock thread + DMs to both parties | Within 30 minutes |

### Moderation as Community Building

Good moderation isn't just about removing bad stuff. It's about reinforcing good stuff. Every moderation action is also a signal to the community about what you value.

- When you thank someone for being helpful, you're encouraging helpfulness.
- When you explain *why* you removed a post, you're teaching the community standards.
- When you publicly recognize a positive contribution, you're shaping culture.

**A practical tip:** Create a #mod-log channel (visible only to mods) where you record every moderation action. This helps you spot patterns, track repeat offenders, and review decisions later.

## Building Your Weekly Content Calendar

Stop deciding what to post each morning. That's a recipe for burnout and inconsistency. Build a weekly content calendar and execute it.

**Here's a template you can steal:**

| Time | Monday (Educational) | Tuesday (Spotlight) | Wednesday (Interactive) | Thursday (Culture) | Friday (Roundup) |
|------|---------------------|---------------------|------------------------|-------------------|------------------|
| Morning | Post tutorial/guide | Post member spotlight | Post poll or prompt | Post behind-the-scenes | Post weekly roundup |
| Afternoon | Respond to comments | Respond to comments | Host Q&A session | Engage in thread | Preview next week |
| Evening | Pin best response | Update spotlight list | Share poll results | Share culture wins | Weekend prompt |

This takes about 30-45 minutes per day. Morning is your "create and post" block. Afternoon is your "engage and respond" block. Evening is your "close and document" block.

## What to Do This Week

1. **Set up your daily themes.** Write Monday through Friday into your content calendar. Assign one theme per day.

2. **Plan your first week of content.** One post per day, five days total. No more. You can scale up later.

3. **Write your community rules (3-5).** Post them in your #welcome or #rules channel. Keep them short and specific.

4. **Set up auto-moderation.** If you're on Discord, configure AutoMod for spam and link filtering. On Circle, set up content moderation filters.

5. **Start your spotlight list.** Open a note or document. Every time a member shares a win, contributes something useful, or helps someone, add their name. You'll use this for Spotlight Tuesday.

6. **Block 30 minutes per day on your calendar.** Same time, every day. This is your community operations block. Don't skip it.
