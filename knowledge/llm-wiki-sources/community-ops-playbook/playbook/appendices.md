## Appendix A: Template Quick-Reference Guide

The 10 core templates (included in both Essential and Complete tiers) and 5 bonus templates (included in Complete tier) are designed to be used together as a complete community operations system. Here's what each one does and when to use it.

### Core Templates

**Community Launch Checklist** — A pre-launch planning tool that covers everything from platform setup and channel design to content preparation and moderator recruitment. Use it 2-4 weeks before opening your doors. Each task is categorized by phase (Foundation, Setup, Content, Launch) with owners and deadlines.

**Member Onboarding Flow** — A 14-day onboarding sequence template that maps out exactly what happens from the moment a member joins. Columns include Day, Action, Channel, and Goal Metric. Fill it in before your first members arrive, then adjust based on what actually works. The goal is to hit a 60%+ onboarding completion rate by day 14.

**Weekly Content Calendar** — A day-by-day content planning sheet organized around the five daily themes (Educational Monday, Spotlight Tuesday, Interactive Wednesday, Culture Thursday, Roundup Friday). Each entry includes the content type, channel, author, and status. Plan one week at a time or batch four weeks at once.

**Engagement Score Tracker** — A member-level scoring sheet that tracks participation across posts, replies, reactions, events attended, and referrals. Each action is weighted by value (posts = 3 pts, replies = 5 pts, events = 20 pts). Use it to identify power users, at-risk members, and VIP candidates. Update weekly for communities under 500 members.

**Member Segmentation Matrix** — A categorization tool that segments members by tier, activity level, interests, and LTV. Use it alongside the Engagement Score Tracker to group members into buckets (New, Active, Engaged, Power User, At Risk) and determine what each group needs next.

**Referral Program Designer** — A planning sheet for your member-get-member program. Track referral sources, rewards offered, conversion rates, and total acquired per channel. Use it to figure out which referral incentives actually drive growth and which sources bring the highest-quality members.

**Event Planning SOP** — An end-to-end event workflow template covering everything from ideation to post-event follow-up. Phases include Planning, Promotion, Execution, and Debrief. Each phase has specific tasks, owners, and timelines. Use it for recurring events (monthly AMAs, workshops) and one-off events (launches, special guests).

**Community KPI Dashboard** — Your central metrics hub. Tracks the five metric categories (Acquisition, Activation, Engagement, Retention, Revenue) with columns for current value, target, trend direction, and action needed. Update once a week. This sheet tells you at a glance whether your community is healthy or heading for trouble.

**Feedback Collection System** — A structured feedback tracking sheet that captures input from member surveys, direct messages, community posts, and churn interviews. Each entry includes the feedback source, priority level, owner, and status. Review weekly and close the loop with members who submitted feedback — even if the answer is "not right now."

**Revenue Tracking Sheet** — A monthly revenue dashboard for paid communities. Tracks MRR, ARPU, new signups, churned members, and revenue by source (subscriptions, courses, coaching, products). Update at the end of each month. Use it to spot revenue trends before they become problems.

### Bonus Templates (Complete Tier Only)

**Community Health Audit** — A quarterly assessment framework that scores your community across five pillars (Growth, Engagement, Retention, Revenue, Community Health). Each pillar gets a 1-10 score with supporting data. Use it every 90 days to produce a one-page summary and a prioritized action list for the next quarter.

**Member Satisfaction Survey (NPS + Qualitative)** — A survey template combining Net Promoter Score with open-ended questions about what members value most and what's missing. Run it quarterly or after major changes. The qualitative responses are often more useful than the NPS number itself.

**SOP Template (Generic)** — A fill-in-the-blank standard operating procedures template for any recurring community process. Use it to document workflows for your own reference or to hand off tasks to moderators, champions, or team members.

**Crisis Management Plan** — A pre-written plan for handling serious incidents in your community: harassment, data breaches, platform outages, PR issues. It includes immediate response steps, communication templates, escalation paths, and a post-incident review process. Fill it out before you need it.

**Growth Experiments Tracker** — A structured experiment log for A/B testing engagement strategies. Columns include Hypothesis, Variable Tested, Duration, Results, and Decision. Use it to track every test you run so you don't repeat failed experiments or forget what worked.

## Appendix B: Glossary of Community Management Terms

**Activation** — The process of getting a new member to their first meaningful experience in the community. Usually measured by onboarding completion rate or time to first interaction.

**ARPU (Average Revenue Per User)** — Total monthly revenue divided by number of paying members. A key metric for understanding whether you're leaving money on the table.

**Champion** — A volunteer member who helps run the community. Champions welcome new members, answer questions, organize events, and provide feedback. They are not employees and are not paid (in most cases). Also called ambassadors or power users.

**Churn** — The rate at which members leave the community. Usually measured monthly for paid communities (percentage of paying members who cancel) and tracked as "inactive member rate" for free communities.

**Cohort** — A group of members who joined during the same time period. Cohort analysis compares behavior across groups (e.g., "did January's members retain better than February's?") to identify the impact of changes to your onboarding or acquisition.

**Conversion Rate** — The percentage of members who move from one tier to the next (free → paid, or paid → VIP). Also used for specific actions like "event registration to attendance."

**DAU/MAU** — Daily Active Users divided by Monthly Active Users. A ratio that measures how many of your monthly members engage on a daily basis. A higher ratio indicates stronger habit formation. 20%+ is considered healthy for most communities.

**Engagement Score** — A composite number that measures a member's overall participation level. Calculated by assigning point values to different actions (posting, replying, attending events, referring others). Used to segment members and identify power users or at-risk members.

**Free Tier** — The no-cost level of access in a tiered community. Provides enough value to demonstrate the community's worth but limited enough to create motivation to upgrade. Usually includes general discussion, welcome channels, and a curated set of free resources.

**K-Factor (Viral Coefficient)** — A metric that measures how many new members each existing member brings in. Calculated as: invites sent per member × invite conversion rate. K > 1 means exponential growth. Most communities operate at K = 0.3 to 0.5.

**LTV (Lifetime Value)** — The total revenue a member generates over their entire time in the community. Used to determine how much you can spend on acquisition and whether your monetization strategy is sustainable.

**MGM (Member-Get-Member)** — A referral program where existing members are incentivized to invite new members. The highest-quality growth channel for most communities, since referred members have higher retention and engagement.

**MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue)** — Total predictable monthly revenue from subscription-based members. The standard metric for paid community health.

**NPS (Net Promoter Score)** — A single-question survey ("How likely are you to recommend this community to a friend?") scored on a 0-10 scale. Used as a high-level satisfaction benchmark. Scores above 50 are considered excellent.

**North Star Metric** — The single metric that best indicates your community's long-term health. Examples: retention rate for paid communities, engagement rate for free communities, time-to-first-resolution for support communities.

**Onboarding** — The process of guiding a new member from their first visit to their first meaningful experience. For most communities, the onboarding window is the first 14 days. Good onboarding is the highest-leverage retention activity you can invest in.

**Paid Tier** — The revenue-generating level of access in a tiered community. Offers full access to channels, exclusive content, events, and direct access to the community operator. Typical price range: $9-99/month depending on community type.

**Retention** — The percentage of members who remain active in the community over a specific time period (30 days, 90 days, 1 year). The most important metric for long-term community health. Improving retention by 10% often has a bigger impact on revenue than doubling acquisition.

**VIP Tier** — The top level of access in a tiered community. Reserved for the most engaged and valuable members. VIP is earned (not purchased) through consistent contribution, helpfulness, and community leadership. Includes direct access to the operator, input on community direction, and exclusive events.

**Viral Loop** — A growth mechanism where existing members bring in new members, who then bring in more members. Examples include shareable content, collaborative events that require bringing a guest, and public showcases of member achievements.

### Tool Stack Recommendations by Community Size

**Tiny (under 100 members):**
- Platform: Discord (free)
- Bots: MEE6 (free) for basic moderation and welcome messages
- Analytics: KPI Dashboard template (free)
- Email: ConvertKit (free tier)
- Payments: Ko-fi or Gumroad
- Total cost: $0-15/month

**Growing (100-1,000 members):**
- Platform: Discord + Circle for paid tier
- Bots: MEE6 premium ($12/mo) + Sesh (free)
- Analytics: Circle Analytics + KPI Dashboard
- Email: ConvertKit ($29/mo) or Mailchimp (free tier)
- Payments: Gumroad ($10/mo) or Stripe
- Total cost: $50-100/month

**Scaling (1,000+ members):**
- Platform: Circle or Skool as primary
- Bots: Arcane ($5/mo) + Ticket Tool ($10/mo)
- Analytics: Common Room (free tier) or Orbit
- Email: ConvertKit ($59/mo) or Loops
- Payments: Stripe or Lemon Squeezy
- Automation: Zapier ($20/mo)
- Total cost: $150-300/month
