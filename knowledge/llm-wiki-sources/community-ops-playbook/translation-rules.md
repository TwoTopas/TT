- ❌ Enterprise WeChat, 企业微信

- ❌ 公众号, Official Accounts
- ❌ 私域, private domain, siyu
- ❌ 小红书, Xiaohongshu, RED

- ❌ 知乎, Zhihu
- ❌ B站, Bilibili
- ❌ 微博, Weibo
- ❌ WeChat Pay, Alipay
- ❌ Red packet, 红包, hongbao
- ❌ Seeded member, 群托, group actor (this is not a Western practice)

## RULE 4: Style Guide — Must Sound Native English

**Tone**: Conversational, direct, knowledgeable. Think "experienced community manager sharing battle-tested tactics."

**Voice**:
- ✅ Use "you" (direct address)
- ✅ Short sentences (average 15-20 words)
- ✅ Bullet points for actionable items
- ✅ Examples with real-sounding numbers (not hypothetical "your mileage may vary")
- ✅ Platform-specific: mention Discord channels, Circle spaces, Skool groups
- ✅ Use contractions: "you'll", "don't", "it's", "they're"

**Forbidden**:
- ❌ Corporate jargon ("leverage", "synergize", "paradigm shift", "holistic")
- ❌ Translation-ese ("It is worth noting that", "need to be considered", "as shown in the table below")
- ❌ Vague claims ("studies show", "research indicates" without sources)
- ❌ Overly formal language ("shall", "heretofore", "utilize")
- ❌ Chinese analogies (no references to Chinese apps, platforms, or cultural concepts)
- ❌ Passive voice where active works better

**Examples**:

✅ GOOD: "New members who complete onboarding in their first week are 4x more likely to stick around for month two. Here's what a good 7-day sequence looks like..."

❌ BAD: "It is necessary to pay attention to the onboarding process, because after research it was found that the new member retention rate is higher when onboarding is completed."

✅ GOOD: "Set up three channels to start: #welcome, #general, and #announcements. You can add more later."

❌ BAD: "The community should establish multiple channels for different functions, such as a welcome channel, a general discussion channel, and an announcement channel."

## RULE 5: Data Integrity — No Fabricated Numbers

- **Do NOT invent statistics, case studies, or research findings.**
- If you need data points, use ONLY these verified sources from our research:
  - Discord: 200M MAU, 94 min/day avg, 26.5M DAU, 19M+ servers
  - TikTok: 1.59B MAU, 4.2% engagement rate by views, 55% Gen Z daily brand engagement
  - Substack: 3M+ paid subscriptions, 5-10% free→paid conversion typical
  - Reddit: 1.1B+ MAU, 100K+ active subreddits
- For examples, use **plausible but clearly labeled as examples**:
  - "A 500-member Discord generating $48K/year" → OK (illustrative example)
  - "Research by [unnamed source] shows 67% of communities..." → NOT OK

## RULE 6: Structure Requirements

Every template and chapter must:
1. **Start with a clear purpose** — what problem does this solve?
2. **Include real-looking sample data** — not empty tables
3. **Have platform notes** — mention Discord/Circle/Skool where relevant
4. **End with a call to action** — what to do next

Every CSV template must:
- Have headers in row 1
- Include column descriptions in row 2 (or as comments)
- Have 10+ rows of sample data starting row 3
- Use realistic but fictional member names/community names
