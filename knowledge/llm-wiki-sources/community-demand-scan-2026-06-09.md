# Community Demand Scan — Reddit "I Wish There Was an App" Analysis
## Date: 2026-06-09
## Source: 9,363 Reddit posts analyzed (Jan 2026)

## Key Findings

### Volume vs Revenue Trap
| Category | Request Volume | Pay Signal | Verdict |
|----------|---------------|------------|---------|
| Productivity | 1,231 (highest) | Low | Crowded, avoid |
| **Finance** | Moderate | **Highest** | ✅ Build for revenue |
| Developer Tools | High | Medium | Complex, technical users |
| Health/Wellness | High (seasonal) | Medium | Q1 spike |

### Anti-Cloud Rebellion
- **7% of all requests** specifically asked for offline-first / privacy-focused tools
- Users cite "subscription fatigue"
- Opportunity: "Buy Once, Use Forever" local tools with iCloud/local network sync

### Frustration Hotspots (highest frustration = best markets)
1. **Developer Platforms** — engineers writing essays about missing AWS/NetSuite features
2. **Parenting** — complex needs for sleep/milestone/school tracking
3. **Cooking** — furious about bloated recipe sites (ads + SEO life stories)
4. **ADHD community** — hyper-specific workflow tools, detailed specs, loyal evangelists

### Trends (Q1 2026)
- **Smart Home Visualization** — people have hardware, hate the software. Demand shifting from devices to data dashboards
- **Health & Wellness** — seasonal New Year spike

### What NOT to Build (data-backed)
- ❌ AI wrappers ("ChatGPT but for X")
- ❌ Generic productivity apps (saturated)
- ❌ Social platforms (fatigue)
- ❌ Crypto/Web3 (<1% of posts)
