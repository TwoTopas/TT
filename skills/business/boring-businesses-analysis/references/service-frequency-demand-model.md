# Service Frequency as Software Demand Predictor

The single best leading indicator of software demand in service industries is **how often the customer is served**.

## The "Notebook Test"

If a service happens **less than once a year**, a paper notebook is a genuinely rational alternative. The operator writes one line per customer per year — that fits in a composition book for a lifetime. If a service happens **monthly or weekly**, manual tracking becomes painful and software has clear value.

## Frequency → Demand Mapper

| Frequency | Software Need | User Complaints | Willingness to Pay | Examples |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|-------------------|----------|
| **Weekly** | Very High | "Can't keep track, losing customers" | $50-150/mo | Pool cleaning, lawn care |
| **Monthly** | High | "Manual billing is killing me" | $40-100/mo | Pest control (some routes) |
| **Quarterly** | Medium-High | "Forget to schedule follow-ups" | $30-80/mo | Pest control (standard), window cleaning |
| **Bi-annual** | Low-Medium | "It's manageable with a calendar" | $20-50/mo | Carpet cleaning, gutter cleaning |
| **Annual** | Low | "Paper is fine, I only see them once" | $20-40/mo | Chimney sweep, HVAC tune-up |
| **2-5 years** | Very Low | "Notebook genuinely works" | <$20/mo | Septic pumping |
