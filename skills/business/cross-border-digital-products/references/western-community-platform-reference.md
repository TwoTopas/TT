## Operational Cadence: Chinese vs Western

**Chinese model**: Relationship-first, hourly schedule, personal 1-on-1 follow
- 7:30-8:30 — Morning greeting + news share
- 11:00 — Product education + social proof
- 13:00-16:00 — Interactive activity
- 17:00-18:00 — Behind-the-scenes content
- 19:30 — Evening value post + product recommendation

**Western model**: Content-first, weekly rhythm, automated onboarding
- Monday: Educational content (tutorials, guides)
- Tuesday: Community spotlight (member wins, showcases)
- Wednesday: Interactive (AMA, Q&A, polls, challenges)
- Thursday: Culture / behind-the-scenes
- Friday: Weekly roundup + weekend preview
- Weekend: Light engagement (off-topic, games)

## Platform-Specific Community Structures

**Discord Server (typical best practice):**
```
#welcome → onboarding, roles, rules
#announcements → admin posts (read-only)
#general → open chat
#introductions → new members
#resources → pinned guides, FAQs
#events → upcoming activities
#feedback → member suggestions
#showcase → member work
[voice channels] → voice chat
```

**Circle Space**: Topic-based spaces, course integration, live streams
**Skool Group**: Gamification (leaderboards, levels), calendar-based, native monetization

## Engagement Tactics: What NOT to transfer from Chinese methodology

| Chinese Tactic | Why NOT to use | Western Replacement |
|----------------|----------------|-------------------|
| Red packets (红包) | Not a Western practice | Welcome discount, free trial |
| Seeded members (群托) | Perceived as deceptive | Ambassador program (transparent) |
| Group buying (拼团) | Different psychology | Referral discount, bring-a-friend |
| QR code chains | WeChat-specific | Referral link, landing page |
| Moments mining (朋友圈) | WeChat-specific | Social listening, content repurposing |
| Hourly group posts | Feels like spam to Western users | Weekly content calendar |
| Personal 1-on-1 follow-up to every member | Unsustainable without WeChat ecosystem | Automated onboarding + segmented outreach |
