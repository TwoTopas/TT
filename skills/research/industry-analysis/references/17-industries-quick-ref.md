## Bottom-up ranking (from research)

Industries fully researched with 11-dimension comparison table. Data sources include DDG search, Reddit community mining, competitor pricing pages, and market reports.

### Top tiers by viability for solo-entrepreneur SaaS

**Tier 1 (strong demand + tech gap):**
1. **Window Cleaning** — 60–75% margins, very high recurring (monthly), <20% software adoption, operators actively seeking CRM on Reddit. Competitors: Opervo $24.99, Jobber $29. Gap: route-based recurring scheduling.
2. **Carpet Cleaning** — 55–70% margins, 50–60K operators, 25–30% software adoption. Competitor ServiceMonster at $89–249 is the de facto standard but expensive. Gap: undercut at $29–49 with industry-specific features.
3. **Pool Cleaning** — 50–65% margins, very high recurring (weekly), 78–125K businesses, ~85% unserved. Skimmer ($49–98) raised prices 2x, angering users. PoolOps ($29.99) validating low-price demand. Gap: chemical compliance reporting.

**Tier 2 (valid demand but execution barriers for China-based dev):**
4. **Pest Control** — 45–55% margins, high recurring (quarterly), 30–35K operators, 15–20% software adoption.
5. **Chimney Sweep** — 60–75% margins, moderate recurring (annual), 5–8K operators, <10% software adoption.

### Demand validation filter

**The "notebook test":** If service frequency >1 year and manual process takes <5 min/record, software adoption will be low regardless of market size.

| Industry | Frequency | Notebook test | SaaS viable? |
