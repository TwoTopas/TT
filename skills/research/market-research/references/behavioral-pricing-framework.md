## ⚠️ Critical Correction: The Decoy Effect

**WRONG (old version of this file):** Lite tier is the decoy.
**CORRECT:** The decoy is the MIDDLE tier, CLOSE in price to the target, and DOMINATED by it.

**Economist experiment (Dan Ariely, Predictably Irrational):**
| Option | Price | What You Get | Role |
|--------|-------|-------------|------|
| Web-only | $59 | Digital access | Low anchor |
| Print-only | $125 | Print only | **DECOY** — same price as Web+Print, strictly worse |
| Web+Print | $125 | Digital + Print | **TARGET** — selected by 84% of buyers |

**The decoy ($125 Print-only) and target ($125 Web+Print) are the SAME PRICE.** That's the key. One is clearly dominated.

For ZIP download products, the pattern is:
| Lite $19 | Standard $39 🚩 | Complete 🎯 $49 |
|-----------|----------------|-----------------|
| 5 templates | 10 templates | 15 templates + playbook + tools |
| Condensed guide | Full guide | Full guide + health score |
| Entry level | Deliberately missing playbook and bonuses — **WHY PAY $39 WITHOUT THE GOOD STUFF?** | Everything. Best value. "Most Popular" |

**The $39 Standard is dominated by $49 Complete** — at only $10 more, you get the playbook, bonus templates, and health score. This is the true decoy effect.
