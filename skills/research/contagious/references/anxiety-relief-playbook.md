# Anxiety-Relief Emotional Playbook

> A worked pattern from The Discord Community Operations Playbook (2026-06-10 session).
> Use this when scoring or improving Emotion dimension on template/playbook/tool/products.

## Core Insight

Template/playbook/tool products cannot score on excitement (awe, amusement, anger).
They CAN score 7-9 on anxiety-relief, which is a HIGH-arousal emotional arc:
anxiety (accumulated tension) → relief (sudden release).

The relief from chronic low-grade anxiety is physiologically similar to exhilaration —
the scoring guide MUST count it as high-arousal.

## The 5-Layer Anxiety-Relief Stack

Applied in order of impact:

### Layer 1: Lead Narrative (Listing / Landing Page)
**Frame the pain as a feeling, not a problem.**
- ❌ "Your community lacks operational systems"
- ✅ "It's 11 PM and you're scrolling through your server one last time before bed. #general is quiet. There's a knot in your stomach."

**Physical sensation anchors to use:**
| Anchor | Counts as | Example |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Sleep disruption | Always strong | "checking Discord at 2 AM", "waking up at 3 AM thinking about...", "sleep through the night" |
| Bodily tension | Always strong | "knot in my stomach", "weight on my chest" |
| Compulsive behavior | Strong | "checking while brushing teeth", "scrolling before bed" |
| Guilt | Moderate | "feeling guilty when not 'on'", "wondering if you're letting people down" |
| Low-grade dread | Strong | "that feeling you're forgetting something", "dreading opening Discord" |

### Layer 2: Testimonials with Emotional Payload
**Don't just report metrics — report relief.**
- ❌ "The templates saved me 10 hours of setup time"
- ✅ "The weight lifted off my chest. For the first time in months, I didn't feel that knot in my stomach. The peace of mind alone was worth the price."

**Structure:**
1. Name the anxiety state ("I was running on coffee and anxiety")
2. Name the physical symptom ("checking every 20 minutes", "waking up at 3 AM")
3. Name the exact relief moment ("the first Saturday I didn't open Discord")
4. Name the emotional outcome ("peace of mind", "quiet confidence", "I finally slept")

### Layer 3: Product Content Delivers the Promise
**The product itself must contain anxiety-relief material — not just the marketing.**
Create actual content within the product that:
- Validates the feeling ("That's not weakness. That's your brain trying to care about something that has no off-switch.")
- Gives a structured relief ritual ("The Weekly Shutdown Ritual — every Friday at 5 PM")
- Includes a self-diagnostic tool ("The Anxiety Inventory — 5 questions, 30 seconds")
- Sets sustainable expectations ("You're not a bad community manager if you step away. You're a bad one if you burn out.")

### Layer 4: Bonus Shareable Assessment
**Add a separate mini-assessment within the product focused on anxiety/burnout.**
- 5 questions, different from the main assessment
- Its own scoring scale and shareable output
- Example: Burnout Risk Score (Hands-Off Hours, 3 AM Test, System Coverage, Delegation Confidence, Recharge Quality)
- Generates a share-ready text block distinct from the main product

### Layer 5: Closing Frame
**End on the emotional outcome, not the feature list.**
- ❌ "Buy now and get organized"
- ✅ "Stop winging it. Start sleeping."

## Implementation Checklist

- [ ] Lead with "the feeling" — at least 3 physical sensation anchors
- [ ] Testimonials name anxiety state + physical symptom + relief moment + emotional outcome
- [ ] Product contains at least one anxiety-relief section (Burnout Prevention, Shutdown Ritual, etc.)
- [ ] Product includes a bonus anxiety/burnout assessment with shareable output
- [ ] Closing line names the emotional outcome (sleep, peace, confidence)

## Scoring Rules

| To score 5+ | To score 7+ | To score 9+ |
|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| Pain→relief arc in listing | Physical sensation anchors (3+) | All 5 layers implemented |
| Before/after narrative | Testimonials carry emotional payload | Product content validates and relieves anxiety |
| | Product contains relief content | Bonus shareable assessment exists |
| | Closing frame is emotional | Closing line is memorable and actionable |
